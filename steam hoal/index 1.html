    Formula 1, also known as Formula 1 or F1, is the highest class of international racing for open-wheel, single-seat Formula One cars sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA). The World Drivers Championship, which became the FIA Formula One World Championship in 1981, has been one of the main forms of racing in the world since its inaugural season in 1950. From formula 1 in The name refers to the set of rules that all participating vehicles must follow. A Formula 1 season consists of a series of races, known as Grands Prix, that take place around the world on purpose-built roads and closed public roads.

    Formula 1 is a traditional term that implies that, within Formula cars, it is the set of rules of the highest technological and mechanical level. In past times there was also Formula 2, Formula 3 and Formula 4; F3 exists as a high-performance training category, while F2 and F4 are categories that work to train a driver to the top. In all these cases the pilot drives the same style of vehicle, without a roof, only one seat and with the wheels uncovered.

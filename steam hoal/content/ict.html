Over the years, fans have also watched Formula 1's use of new technologies. Recently, hybrid power units have become a game changer for F1. Before hybrid electric units were introduced in Formula 1 cars in 2014, The average thermal efficiency of cars on the road was around 30%. This means that only a third of the car's gasoline is used to power it.

High speed of F1 wireless technology:

In 2017, F1 teams started using a system of two wireless technologies: multigigabit Wi-Fi and 5 GHz. This technology allows an F1 car to transmit data wirelessly as it moves through a tunnel lane. When the car is 4 meters close to the pitlane it starts transferring data much faster, with download speeds of up to 1.9 GB per second. This means that a gigabyte of data takes less than five seconds to transfer.

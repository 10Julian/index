How math took over F1

F1's telemetry system continuously collects data on all sorts of variables in racing cars: oil and water levels, clutch fluid pressure, G-forces and engine revolutions per minute. This information is communicated to engineers in pits and in control centers using a variety of radio and wireless technologies.

Aside from this external data from the car such as temperature, air pressure and humidity. The numbers collected on both vehicle and climate from the obvious parts of a large equation, the sum of which will indicate which lap the rider is on in the pit and what adjustments will be made to the car.

F1 is the most data-intensive sport in the world, and math influences race and championship results more strongly than other sports. As a result, developers of new technologies will bet very high to make the process of data mining and calculating the required sums more complex and efficient.

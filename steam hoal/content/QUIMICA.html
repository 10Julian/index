Gasoline

Gasoline is a liquid hydrocarbon that has an approximate density of 850 kg/m3 and is fundamentally composed of 75% saturated hydrocarbons or paraffins, and 25% aromatic hydrocarbons. The most common chemical formula is C12H26 (12 carbon atoms to 26 hydrogen).

In Formula 1, the fuel does not contain the chemical additives responsible for adapting to daily use that are required of conventional gasoline.

Although it has 99% of the properties of conventional gasoline, in that remaining 1% is the main responsible for the explosion, for the chemical reaction that generates almost 19,000 revolutions per minute, a figure that a conventional propellant is unable to withstand.
